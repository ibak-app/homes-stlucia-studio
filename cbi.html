Frequently Asked Questions

Saint Lucia's Citizenship by Investment Programme was established under the Citizenship by Investment Act No. 14 of 2015. It allows foreign investors to obtain citizenship through a qualifying investment, including real estate purchases of US$200,000+ (shared) or US$300,000+ (sole ownership) in CIU-approved developments.
The typical processing time is 3-6 months from submission. This includes due diligence checks, background verification, and administrative processing by the CIU. Expedited processing may be available for an additional fee.
Yes. Your application can include your spouse, dependent children under 30, and dependent parents over 55. Additional government processing fees apply per dependent. The entire family receives Saint Lucia citizenship and passports.
Non-nationals purchasing property in Saint Lucia must obtain an Alien Landholding Licence. The fee is typically 10% of the property value. For CBI applicants, the licence process is streamlined and often handled concurrently with the citizenship application.
After the 5-year holding period, you may sell the property on the open market. Your citizenship is permanent and is not affected by the sale. The property may be resold to another CBI applicant, making it an attractive investment for future buyers.
